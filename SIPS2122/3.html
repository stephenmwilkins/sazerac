SAZERAC presents:

Machine Learning for Understanding Cosmic Dawn and the Epoch of Reionisation

Date: 2-3 February

Time: TBC

SOC: Michele Bianco (EPFL), Sambit Giri (University of Zurich), Adrian Liu (McGill University), Pratika Dayal (University of Groningen), Steven Murray (Arizona State University)

Location: Zoom, YouTube, and Slack

The Cosmic Dawn (CD) and the Epoch of Reionisation (EoR) are key periods of transition in the early Universe witnessing the formation of both primordial structures and the first photon radiating sources. These epochs mark an essential period for understanding gravity and the processes that led to the ultimate transition of the intergalactic medium (IGM) from cold and neutral to hot and ionised.

A series of ongoing and future experiments are set to revolutionise the exploration of the CD and EoR. For instance, radio astronomy is entering a golden era with the help of present and future radio interferometric telescopes, such as Low-frequency Array (LOFAR), Murchison Widefield Array (MWA), Hydrogen Epoch of Reionization Array (HERA) and in particular the Square Kilometre Array (SKA), for the detection of the 21-cm signal that is produced by neutral hydrogen in the IGM during the CD and EoR. At the same time, a series of earth- and space-based telescopes such as the Subaru telescope, the James Webb Space Telescope (JWST) and the Euclid telescope aim to unveil the properties of the galaxies present during these times. Moreover, over the next decade, these experiments will be supplemented with observations from the Extremely Large Telescope (ELT) and the Nancy Grace Roman Space Telescope (NGRST). The wealth of data produced by these observations will propel high-redshift cosmology into the data-driven regime.

The scale and complexity of these observations will require robust methods to analyse them. In the last decades, artificial neural networks and deep learning algorithms have been developed to face the challenge of big data in modern science. Recently, several works in the literature have explored the usage of machine learning techniques in various contexts of cosmology and astrophysics.

During this SAZERAC two-day SIP meeting, we would like to discuss the state of various machine learning applications in the context of CD and EoR. The goal of this meeting is to investigate the applicability of this technology in domains such as:

  • Astrophysical parameter estimation
  • Constraining the reionisation history
  • Removal of contamination in observations, such as noise and foreground signals
  • Feature identification in 21-cm image data
  • Emulators for 21cm and/or radiative transfer simulations
  • Detecting Lyα emitters
  • Interpreting absorption lines in quasar spectra
