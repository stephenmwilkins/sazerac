SAZERAC: Steves' Summer All Zoom Epoch of Reionization Astronomy Conference

Dates

With many summer conferences cancelled due to the Covid-19 pandemic we have decided to organise a remote conference, SAZERAC, focussing on the epoch of reionization. The meeting will be delivered entirely over Zoom, though may also be live streamed and recorded. Abstract submission is open to all however will will prioritise finishing (final or penultimate year) PhD students and junior post-docs when selecting talks.

What is a Sazerac?

The Sazerac is a local New Orleans variation of a cognac or whiskey cocktail, named for the Sazerac de Forge et Fils brand of cognac brandy that served as its original main ingredient.[1] The drink is most traditionally a combination of cognac or rye whiskey, absinthe, Peychaud's Bitters, and sugar, although bourbon whiskey or Herbsaint are sometimes substituted.

Ingredients:
  • 5 cl cognac
  • 1 cl absinthe
  • One sugar cube
  • Two dashes Peychaud's Bitters
  • Lemon peel for garnish
Preparation:

Rinse a chilled old-fashioned glass with the absinthe, add crushed ice, and set it aside. Stir the remaining ingredients over ice and set it aside. Discard the ice and any excess absinthe from the prepared glass, and strain the drink into the glass. Add the lemon peel for garnish.

Non-alcoholic Sazerac

For a non-alcoholic alternative the cognac and absinthe can be substituted for non-alcoholic alternatives such as those produced by Lyres. Alternatively you could try this experimenting with falvoured teas and star anise alongside the bitters and sugar.
